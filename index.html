MY JOURNEY

What started off as a plan to get out of the Bombay bubble turned into the best life experience. 15 year old me discovered the joy of learning, and wanted to chase after this new found passion. As I read more, I understood more, and the world began to open up to me. This excitement from learning (not synonymous with studying), was one I hadn't felt before, and I was determined to continue down this path. After much research, and self introspection to choose 'this path', I decided studying abroad in the United States was the ideal choice. The combination of a research based insitution, flexibility to try out a variety of different subjects, and tempurpedic beds was why I chose WashU. And I never looked back.


"Great people do things before they're ready. They do things before they know they can do it. Doing what you're afraid of, getting out of your comfort zone, taking risks like that--that is what life is. You might find out something about yourself that's really special. If you're not good, who cares? You tried something. Now you know something about yourself." - Amy Poehler

HIGHLIGHTS
from the past 4 years

Contrary to popular belief, I did more than sleep in the tempurpedic mattresses and eat world class dining hall food in college. Here is a snapshot of the things I am most proud of these past four years.

2014, Freshman Year: the year of firsts


- Got my first article published for Armour- a fashion magazine
- Got my own radio show and DJ'ed every week.
- Braved my first winter (biggest accomplishment of the year)
- Attended my first Thanksgiving!
- Went to my first college party
- Got my first paycheck
- Wrote and published two articles for Vogue Magazine
- Started learning a foriegn language- Spanish

2015, Sophomore Year: the year of discovery and finding my path


- Selected to attend Leadershape camp, which was an impactful and in some ways, life changing.
- Decided to switch to the Olin Business School, and pursue a major in Entreprenuership.
- Completed my first case competition - and won an honorable mention for it
- Pitched a busienss idea for a cheaper alternative to textbooks called NowBooks, and created a business plan for it over 6 months.
- Was one of two students to score a dinner invitation with top Investor Mark Suster, and musician Chamillionaire.
- Undertook a marketing focused consulting project for Hollywood Casino in St. Louis
- Made it on to the Dean's List for my academic honors
- Consulted with Safetrek, a personal safety application, and crafted innovative strategies for them to enter new markets with their product.
- Negotiated my first business deal at Scootsy Internship
- Joined a sorority, and made a family in College.
- Commited myself to a lifestyle of health and fitness

2016, Junior Year: the year of taking chances and saying more ‘yes’


- Studied abroad in Sydney, Hong Kong and Thailand
- Interned with 21 Century Fox in Hong Kong
- Landed my dream internship in a Capgemini's Innovation Exchange in San Francisco
- Built my first robot that came second in a robo-battle
- Wrote a thesis on 'The Role of Social Networking Sites on Intercultural Adjustment of International Students' by collecting data and conducting first-hand research.
- Participated in my first hackathon
- Built my first functional website

2017, Senior Year: the year that everything that culminated together in perfect harmony!


- Completed my Entreprenuership Major, Computer Science and International Business Minors
- Built my first functional Mobile App
- Created an Internet of Things connected garage door using hardware, with a supported remote, mobile app and website.
- Created a mobile app that helps respond to anxiety related symptoms by offering real time ways to cope
- Tutored underclassmen in Microeconomics, and discovered a newfound passion for teaching.
- Went on my first trip with friends to Vegas and LA
- Built a website that analyzes social media statistics for restaurants
- Designed, wrote a story and completely coded a fully functional, 3D, Multi level video game
- Took a personal training course from the American College of Sports Medicine to further my learning in the realm of fitness
- Took the best class of my college career: Positive Psychology (which may be the secret to acheiving this harmony in senior year)
- Created this website!
